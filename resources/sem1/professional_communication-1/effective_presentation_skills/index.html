Effective Presentation Skills

Public Speaking

  • Speeches and Presentations are essential part of business communication.
  • Every day business executives and employees have to make formal as well as informal speeches.
  • Executives have to make speeches at company meetings, seminars, conferences, etc.
  • Sales employees have to make hundreds of mini-presentations to persuage the potential customers to buy.
  • Speeches and presentations involve the same fundamental principles of oral communications and provide the advantage of influencing people with enthusiasn and apparent confidence which written document cannot.
  • But compared with presentations, speeches are more formal and are delivered more on formal occasions. Presentations are usually short and less formal. These are delivered with demonstrations of audio-visual aids followed by answers to questions from the audience.

Speeches

Characteristics of a good speech

  1. Clarity → The speaker should be clear in his ideas and he should have a good command over language to express his ideas clearly so that they are instantly understood by the listeners.
  2. Length of speech → It should neither be too breif nor too long. If the speech is too long, it will fail to sustain the interest of the audience for a long time. If it is too brief it won't hold the attention of the listeners.
  3. It should be informative → so that the audience can gain something from it. If the speaker is repetitive and dwells at length on the points already known to the listeners, he will simply bore the audience.
  4. It should be interesting → If the speaker makes an appeal to the emotions of the listeners he can easily win their hearts over to his side, and if the speeh appeals to the head also, it will have the most desired effect.
  5. Should convery conrete facts rather than abstract ideas → a speaker should convery briefly and clearly his ideas to the listeners.

How to make an effective speech?

Delivery of a good speech involves two steps. (1) The preparation for the speech. (2) The delivery of the Speech.

  1. Preparation for the speech → preparation for the specch involves not only organisation of relevant thoughts but also careful selection of thoughts.
    • The speaker must exclude irrelevant thoughts from the draft of the speech.
    • The next step for the speakers is to decide upon the language in which he is to express his thoughts. He should prepare the whole speech before hand.
  2. Delivery of the speech → The second step is the delivery of the speech. Once the preparation has been, made the opening of the speech is very important.
    • The speaker must appeal to the hearts as well as heads of the listerners and create interest in their minds.
    • He may open the topic with a striking quotation, he must emphasize how the topic affects the vital interests of the audience.
    • He should vary the pitch of his voice according to the point to emphasized.
    • Eye contact with the audience should be made.
    • An important point to be discussed is the length of the speech. The speech should neither be too long nor too short.
    • Closing of a speech is the most strategic part and should be carefully made. Towards the close the speaker must summarize, restate and outline briefly the main points of the speech. The closing may be wih a befiting verse, a biblical quotation or a relevant joke but it must reflect the profundity and earnestness of the speaker.

Guidelines for delivering speech

  1. In the start of the speech → the opening of the talk is highly important. The speaker has to create interest in the minds of the audience. After formally addressing them, he can gain the immediate attention of his audience by :-
    • Raising a question
    • Narrating a related interesting story
    • Opening with a striking quotation.
    • Telling how the topic affects the vitals interest of the audience.
    • Highlighting the shocking facts.
  2. During speech
    • One should put one's heart into the talking. It should look as if speech is not flowing out of the mind but also out of the heart of speaker.
    • Stress the important words.
    • Vary the pitch of voice flowing up and down the scale from high to low and back again.
    • Establish and maintain eye contact with the audience.
    • Speak loudly enough to be heard.
  3. At the end of the speech → the close of a specch, being most strategic part, should be made carefully and in accordance with planning in advance.
