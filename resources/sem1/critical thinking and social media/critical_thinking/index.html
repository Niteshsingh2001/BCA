Critical Thinking

Critical Thinking

Definition → the ability to apply a clear, rational thought process using reflective and independent thinking for new knowledge.

Features of Critical Thinking

  • Evaluate the evidence :
    • Critical thinking examines, compares, judges and establishes the validity of evidence.
  • Ask questions :
    • Question information collected and the resulting conclusions, and ask the right questions to determine if data is accurate and unbiased.
  • Think analytically :
    • Examine the different parts of something in order to understand or explain it.
  • Conceptualize ideas :
    • Conceptualizing is the understanding and ability to form an idea.
    • It brings together observation, experience and data through mental imagery.
  • Draw Inferences :
    • To infer is to "read between the lines".
    • People give meaning to and make sound assumptions through what they experience and learn.
    • When you infer, you examine prior experience and knowledge and combine them with suggested facts to arrive at a new belief or course of action.
  • Keep an Open Mind :
    • Open-mindedness is characterzied by being receptive to other views and having the willingness to change views with new facts and evidence.
    • Open-mindness increases intellectual capabilities by allowing for new experiences and perspective.
  • Synthesize information :
    • Synthesis combines pieces of information and ideas and recasts them to form something original.
    • In sysnthesis, you examine how information is connected.
    • As you understand each piece of information spearately, you enhance your understanding of them as a whole and can thus form new insight.

Why is critical thinking important?

  • Critical thinking is important for making judgements about sources of information and forming your own arguments.
  • Critical thinking is important in all disciples and throughout all stages of the research process.
